## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "HIPAA"** means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (Pub. L. 104-191), as amended, including the HIPAA Rules promulgated thereunder (45 CFR Parts 160, 162, and 164), and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH Act), as amended.

**1.2 "Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §160.103 and means individually identifiable health information that is transmitted or maintained in any form or medium (paper, electronic, or verbal) that relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health condition of an individual; the provision of health care to an individual; or the past, present, or future payment for the provision of health care to an individual, and that identifies the individual or with respect to which there is a reasonable basis to believe the information can be used to identify the individual.

**1.3 "Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted in electronic form.

**1.4 "Covered Entity"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §160.103 and, for purposes of this Agreement, means MedData Nexus Health Systems.

**1.5 "Business Associate"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §160.103 and, for purposes of this Agreement, means [VENDOR LEGAL NAME] as described in this Agreement.

**1.6 "Breach"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §164.402 and means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI, subject to the exceptions set forth in 45 CFR §164.402.

**1.7 "Designated Record Set"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §164.501.

**1.8 "Minimum Necessary"** refers to the standard under 45 CFR §164.502(b) requiring that access to, use, or disclosure of PHI be limited to the minimum amount necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

**1.9 "Security Incident"** has the meaning set forth in 45 CFR §164.304 and means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

**1.10 "Subcontractor"** means a person to whom Business Associate delegates a function, activity, or service, other than in the capacity of a member of the Business Associate's workforce, involving the creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of PHI.

## ARTICLE 2: OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

### 2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate may create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI only as necessary to perform the services described in the Underlying Agreement ("Permitted Purposes"). Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate HIPAA if done by the Covered Entity. Specifically, Business Associate may:

(a) Use PHI internally for the Permitted Purposes;

(b) Disclose PHI to Covered Entity upon request;

(c) Use PHI for the proper management and administration of Business Associate's operations, or to carry out the legal responsibilities of Business Associate, provided that: (i) the disclosure is required by law; or (ii) Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the PHI will be held confidentially and used or further disclosed only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed to the recipient, and the recipient will notify Business Associate of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of the PHI has been breached;

(d) Use PHI to provide Data Aggregation services to Covered Entity relating to the health care operations of Covered Entity, as permitted by 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(i)(B);

(e) Report violations of law to appropriate federal and state authorities, consistent with 45 CFR §164.502(j)(1).

### 2.2 Prohibited Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate shall not:

(a) Use or disclose PHI other than as permitted by this Agreement, the Underlying Agreement, or as required by law;

(b) Use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate HIPAA if done by Covered Entity;

(c) Sell PHI or use PHI for marketing purposes without Covered Entity's written authorization;

(d) Use PHI to train machine learning models, improve AI systems, or for product development purposes without prior written authorization from Covered Entity's CISO. This prohibition applies even if PHI is de-identified prior to such use — de-identification does not constitute authorization under this provision;

(e) Share PHI with Subcontractors without complying with the requirements of Article 4.

### 2.3 Minimum Necessary Standard

Business Associate shall make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, or request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose, consistent with 45 CFR §164.502(b).

## ARTICLE 3: SAFEGUARD REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 HIPAA Security Rule Compliance

Business Associate shall implement and maintain administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted on behalf of Covered Entity, as required by 45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C (Security Standards for the Protection of Electronic Protected Health Information).

### 3.2 Required Safeguards

Without limiting the generality of Section 3.1, Business Associate shall:

(a) **Access Controls:** Implement policies and procedures for electronic information systems that maintain ePHI to allow access only to authorized persons or software programs (45 CFR §164.312(a)(1));

(b) **Audit Controls:** Implement hardware, software, and/or procedural mechanisms to record and examine activity in information systems that contain or use ePHI (45 CFR §164.312(b));

(c) **Transmission Security:** Implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI transmitted over electronic communications networks (45 CFR §164.312(e)(1));

(d) **Encryption:** Encrypt ePHI in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher and at rest using AES-256 or equivalent;

(e) **Workforce Training:** Provide HIPAA security awareness training to all workforce members with access to ePHI on an annual basis;

(f) **Risk Analysis:** Conduct an accurate and thorough assessment of the potential risks and vulnerabilities to ePHI it holds on behalf of Covered Entity on an annual basis, and make results available to Covered Entity upon request;

(g) **Incident Response:** Maintain documented incident response procedures that include notification obligations to Covered Entity as described in Article 5.

### 3.3 Audit Rights

Upon reasonable notice (minimum 30 days, unless a security incident requires immediate access), Covered Entity may audit Business Associate's compliance with this Agreement and HIPAA Security Rule requirements. Business Associate shall provide requested documentation, access to relevant systems (subject to reasonable confidentiality protections), and personnel cooperation within 10 business days of a written audit request.

## ARTICLE 5: BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 5.1 Breach Discovery and Notification

**5.1.1** Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of a Breach of Unsecured PHI within **sixty (60) calendar days** of discovery of the Breach, as required by 45 CFR §164.410. Business Associate shall be deemed to have discovered a Breach as of the first day on which any employee, officer, or agent of Business Associate (other than the individual committing the Breach) knew or, by exercising reasonable diligence, should have known of the Breach.

**5.1.2 Expedited notification:** Notwithstanding the 60-day period, Business Associate shall provide initial notification to Covered Entity as soon as practicable and no later than **10 business days** after discovery, sufficient to allow Covered Entity to assess its regulatory notification obligations. Full notification as specified in Section 5.2 shall follow within the 60-day period.

**5.1.3** Notification shall be provided to: legal@meddata-nexus.fake and ciso@meddata-nexus.fake simultaneously, and by phone to Covered Entity's Privacy Officer within 24 hours for any Breach potentially involving more than 500 individuals.

### 5.2 Breach Notification Content

Notification of a Breach shall include, to the extent reasonably possible:

(a) The identification of each individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed;

(b) A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery;

(c) A description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved (e.g., name, date of birth, MRN, diagnosis);

(d) The steps taken or underway to investigate the Breach, mitigate harm, and prevent future occurrences;

(e) Contact information for individuals at Business Associate responsible for managing the Breach response.

### 5.3 Security Incident Reporting

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity's Security Officer any Security Incident of which it becomes aware, including successful and unsuccessful attempts to access, use, or disclose, modify, or destroy PHI, within **30 calendar days** of discovery. This reporting obligation applies to security incidents that do not meet the Breach definition, including attempted intrusions and anomalous access patterns.

## ARTICLE 6: INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS

**6.1 Access:** Business Associate shall provide PHI maintained in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an individual in response to a request for access under 45 CFR §164.524, within 30 calendar days of Covered Entity's written request.

**6.2 Amendment:** Business Associate shall amend PHI as directed by Covered Entity to accommodate requests for amendment under 45 CFR §164.526.

**6.3 Accounting of Disclosures:** Business Associate shall maintain records of disclosures of PHI and provide information to Covered Entity sufficient to permit Covered Entity to respond to requests for an accounting of disclosures under 45 CFR §164.528, for a period of six years from the date of the disclosure.

**6.4 Direct Response to Individuals:** Upon written direction from Covered Entity, Business Associate shall respond directly to an individual's request for access to or amendment of PHI.

## ARTICLE 7: TERMINATION AND DATA RETURN / DESTRUCTION

### 7.1 Termination for Cause

Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement and the Underlying Agreement immediately upon written notice if Business Associate has violated a material term of this Agreement and has failed to cure such violation within 30 days of receiving written notice from Covered Entity specifying the violation.

### 7.2 Return or Destruction of PHI Upon Termination

**7.2.1** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Business Associate shall, at Covered Entity's election, either return or destroy all PHI that Business Associate maintains in any form, and shall retain no copies.

**7.2.2** Business Associate shall complete the return or destruction within **30 days** of the termination date and provide Covered Entity with written certification of destruction or confirmation of return within 10 business days of completion.

**7.2.3** Destruction shall comply with NIST SP 800-88 guidelines (media sanitization) for ePHI.

**7.2.4** If Business Associate determines that returning or destroying PHI is infeasible (e.g., due to integration with other data), Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity and limit further uses and disclosures of PHI to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible. This limited continuation of the Agreement shall survive termination solely for this purpose.
