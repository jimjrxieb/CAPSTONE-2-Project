## What To Master

Clients are at different maturity levels. The consultant's job is to identify the current stage and move them forward with evidence.

## Maturity Curve

| Stage | What It Looks Like | Consultant Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Ad Hoc | personal accounts, shadow AI, no policy | discover usage and reduce immediate exposure |
| Emerging | pilots, early policy, inconsistent controls | define approved paths and data boundaries |
| Scaling | cross-team workflows, governance forming | validate controls and monitor exceptions |
| Mature | lifecycle governance, metrics, red-team cadence | continuous improvement and executive reporting |

## Questions I Should Ask

- Are employees already using unapproved AI tools?
- What approved AI tools exist?
- Who can approve new AI use cases?
- What is the process to move from pilot to production?
- What metrics show safe adoption?
- What incidents or near misses have happened?
- What evidence would leadership use to approve scale?

## What I Need To Know

Do not shame the client for being early.

Say:

> Your current state appears to be Emerging. The practical goal is not to become Mature overnight. The goal is to close the highest-risk gaps before expanding usage.
